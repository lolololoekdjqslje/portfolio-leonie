About Me

I’m Léonie Moreau, a French student at the University of Edinburgh, where I study Philosophy and Politics. My camera is how I explore reality arround me. One of my photographs was shown at The Glasgow Gallery of Photography’s “Street” exhibition in November 2024.

I chose the title Fragmentseach image is a brief intersection of light and circumstance.

My portfolio spans two complementary styles:
• Black & White
• Colour

I shoot digital, focusing on capturing the interplay of light and shadow.

Feel free to reach out on Instagram! @leoniem.photographer

One of my favorite songs at the moment : Who's crying now - Journey. The song Who’s Crying Now by Journey explores the emotional tension between strength and vulnerability, love and loss, echoing the dualities I try to capture in my photography. Its haunting melody and shifting moods mirror how I approach light and shadow in my project Fragments, where each photo reflects a fleeting piece of reality caught between clarity and mystery. Just like the song, my work sits in the space between what’s revealed and what remains hidden.

My Color Palette

This color palette is inspired by the primary colors, blue, red, and yellow which are the foundation of all visual creation, just like light and shadow are for photography. It also reflects the vibrant world of Pac-Man, one of my favorite games. Its playful aesthetic and nostalgic energy continue to inspire me creatively.
